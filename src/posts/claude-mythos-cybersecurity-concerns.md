April 8, 2026

I read something today that left me with a lingering dread. The kind that settles in your gut and doesn't go away when you close the tab.

As [reported](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/07/technology/anthropic-claims-its-new-ai-model-mythos-is-a-cybersecurity-reckoning.html) by _The New York Times_, Anthropic has developed a new AI model called Claude Mythos so powerful that they're choosing not to release it publicly. Instead, they're granting access to a select group of major tech companies to identify and address security vulnerabilities. While that sounds responsible, the details are what I find unsettling.

This model isn't just good at writing code; it excels at breaking it. It can scan for and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities that even the original developers are unaware of. Reportedly, it has already discovered thousands of these vulnerabilities across major operating systems and browsers.

This is where the dread originates. A model like this won't be contained indefinitely. It will inevitably fall into the hands of malicious actors.

Like most people, my life is now largely digital. My finances, communication, personal history, and much more reside online. We trust that infrastructure, largely because we have little alternative. However, reading about Claude Mythos made me realize how fragile that trust is and how easily it could be shattered if such a capable tool were weaponized.

Additional reading: M.G. Siegler, [The Casual Catastrophe of AI](https://spyglass.org/claude-mythos-quandry/)
